Storage and Collaboration

This houses a group of capabilities for you to upload, save & store, audit, comment, and collaborate on your model. You can also export and/or run further analysis on the baseline, comparison, and the simulated data series of the ValQ model.

#Upload the ValQ model to your Workspace

Uploading the model facilities various collaboration functionalities such as saving the model to workspace, adding comments, conducting audit trail, sharing scenarios, model merge and exporting the model as a datasource to be used for other visuals or further analysis.

Here are the steps to upload the ValQ model to your workspace

#1. Login to workspace

Login to the your workspace to see the 'Upload' button and your profile icon at the bezel.



#2. Upload the model to save

In the screenshot model, lets assume you delete a node Ryan Smith


Lets also assume you simulate on the node Chris Morris


You can save these changes at the workspace by clicking 'Upload' near your profile icon, naming your model, and clicking 'upload' at the popup window.


You're model has been uploaded!

Now you can see additional icons at the bezel such as Save, Comments, and the 3 dotsÂ ... having Export as Datasource, History log, and Settings options.

#Add Comments

You can comment on the changes you have made by clicking the 'Comment' icon on top of the node. After posting, your comment is saved on the comment section under the node hierarchy alongside your username and time log.


The users part of the workspace will be able to view and add comments.


Alternatively, you can check the comment log for the entire model by clicking 'Comments' icon at the bezel. Comments are displayed at a Scenario-Node level.

#History Log

As you go to additional options and click 'History Log', you can see the model and scenario level changes made in a chronological order. This feature helps with auditing the ValQ model.

#Collaboration

Collaboration helps to share your model changes, simulations, and comments to all the users under the same workspace. You have to Save your model before collaborating with other users. Assuming a wokspace 'Member' simulates on the existing scenario, creates a new scenario, deletes another node, comments on the changes made, and saves the model.

Collaboration in ValQ lets you view the member's changes, simulations, and comments.

The changes at a Model/Scenario level are captured at the History Log. The comments between the users are displayed at a Node-Scenario level under the 'Comments' icon.
